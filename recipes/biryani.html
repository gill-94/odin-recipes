Vegg Biryani

Description

This dish resembles a vibrant plate of vegetable biryani, where long-grain rice is cooked with a rich blend of spices, tomatoes, and aromatic herbs. The rice is evenly coated in a reddish masala, likely made from ingredients like onions, garlic, ginger, chili, and tomato paste. Fresh green onions sprinkled on top add a mild crunch and a hint of freshness, balancing the warm, spicy flavors of the dish. The grains appear separate and fluffy, which is key to a well-made biryani.

To prepare this style of biryani, you typically sauté whole spices such as bay leaf, cinnamon, and cloves in oil, then add onions, ginger-garlic paste, and chopped tomatoes to create a thick masala base. Mixed vegetables (like carrots, beans, peas, or paneer as seen here) are cooked in the masala before partially cooked basmati rice is layered or mixed in. The dish is then simmered (often on low heat, called “dum” cooking) so the rice absorbs all the flavors. Garnish with fresh herbs like coriander or spring onions before serving for added aroma and color.

Steps

  1. Prepare the rice
    2.

    Wash basmati rice 2–3 times and soak for about 20 minutes. Boil water with salt and cook the rice until it’s about 70–80% done (slightly firm). Drain and keep aside.

  2. Make the masala base
    3.

    Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom). Then add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and cook until soft and oily.

  3. Add vegetables
    4.

    Put in mixed vegetables (and paneer if using). Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, salt, and a little garam masala. Mix well and cook for 5–7 minutes. Add a few spoons of yogurt and cook until everything blends nicely.

  4. Combine rice and masala
    5.

    Add the partially cooked rice on top of the masala. You can either mix lightly or keep it layered. Sprinkle fresh coriander and mint leaves.

  5. Dum cooking (final step)
    6.

    Cover the pan tightly and cook on low heat for 10–15 minutes so the flavors combine. (You can place a heavy lid or even put a pan underneath to avoid burning.)

  6. Serve
    7.

    Gently fluff the rice and serve hot. Add lemon juice, fried onions, or spring onions on top for extra taste.
